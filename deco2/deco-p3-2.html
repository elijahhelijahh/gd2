The Art of Decoupage











Paper Tole (3D Decoupage)

Materials

  1. Prints made specifically for paper tole

    --OR--

    At least 3 paper copies of your own design

  2. Scissors or precision cutting tools

  3. Embossing tool and pad

  4. Foam tape

  5. Adhesive

    • Mod Podge, PVA glue, or any glue that can be used for traditional decoupage

  6. Paint to apply adhesive

How to: Paper Tole (3D Decoupage)

  1. Carefully cut out the shapes, starting with the full base shape. Stay organized because each shape goes in a specific place!

  2. Use adhesive to stick the base shape on your desired surface like you would for traditional decoupage.

  3. Cut out and emboss each piece from the back so that it pops out instead of staying flat. Emboss the areas you need to be flat from the front.

  4. If you’re using a premade paper tole design, the pieces will be marked in order and by section for you. Follow that order, but if you’re using several copies of your own design, you’ll need to decide on how you want to cut and layer your pieces based on which parts you want to be more or less elevated.

  5. Generously cut and stick foam tape to the back of your cut-out pieces. Make sure no foam tape is peaking out.

  6. Stick each piece in order and by section, aligning them above their identical part of the design.

  7. These pieces can be framed as they are,

    8. OR

    If it’ll be exposed (like on a card or envelope) apply a coat of mod podge or whatever adhesive you’re using to help stabilize your piece.
