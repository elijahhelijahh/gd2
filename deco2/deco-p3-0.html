The Art of Decoupage











Traditonal Decoupage

Materials

  1. Base coat

    (Be sure to use white or a light colour because it’ll likely show through, especially if you use a thinner material like tissue paper.)

    • Chalk paint (good for plastic or glass)

    • Acrylic paint also works well for pretty much any surface.

  2. Paint brush and a container of water to clean it

  3. Adhesive
    • Mod Podge is most common.
    • PVA glue or specialty decoupage glues can also be used.

      • These can also be used as a top coat, but you can also use a separate top coat. Consider if you want a matte or shiny top coat, also crackle, satin, etc.

  4. Material(s) you’re going to cover your surface with, this can be:
    • Napkins
    • Wrapping paper
    • Scrapbooking paper
    • Tissue paper
    • Magazine clippings
    • Personal photos
    • Maps
    • Newspaper
    • Book pages
    • Sewing patterns
    • Fabric

      • And the list goes on. If you’re using an unconventional material, however, be sure to research the proper adhesives and top coat.

  5. Scissors or a blade to cut your material

  6. Damp cloth or paper towel to clean up excess adhesive

  7. Sand paper or a sanding sponge (fine or ultrafine, typically 400 grit)

How to: Traditional Decoupage

  1. Clean your surface and let it dry. In most cases, a damp cloth is sufficient.

  2. Paint an undercoat on the area that you’re planning on decoupaging so your materials will stick and show properly. Paint another coat if necessary, drying in between.

  3. Cut your material to the size of your project.

  4. Apply a light coat of your chosen adhesive to both the back of your paice of material and your surface, working in sections. Applying too much can cause bubbles and wrinkles.

  5. Continue until you completely cover your object. Let it dry completely. (Typically at least 15-20 minutes.)

  6. Seal the whole project with your chosen top coat, applying several coats and letting it dry between each coat. Use an ultrafine sanding tool to remove any rough texture every few coats. The more coats you add, the better the inlaid effect will be. Adding at least 3 layers of top coat is recommended.
